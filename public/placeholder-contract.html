Provider Group Professional Services Agreement

For Telehealth Business

THIS PROVIDER GROUP PROFESSIONAL SERVICES AGREEMENT (this “Agreement”) is entered into as of the date on which the Business clicks to accept this Agreement (the “Effective Date”), by and between the licensed professional entity identified in its Bask Health platform profile (“Provider”), and the telehealth business accepting this Agreement through the Bask Health platform (“Business”). Provider and Business may be individually referred to herein as “Party” and collectively referred to herein as the “Parties.”

Background

WHEREAS, Provider is a professional entity employing duly licensed healthcare professionals who provide medical services, including telemedicine and prescription management;

WHEREAS, Business is a telehealth business providing a direct-to-consumer telehealth website (the “Website”) with interfaces for onboarding, for connecting to healthcare professionals and order fulfillment, and for receiving product support;

WHEREAS, both Parties utilize Bask Health, Inc., a third-party software platform for certain telehealth integrations (“Bask”); and

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to integrate via Bask and engage in a service agreement to enable to provision of medical services by Provider to the Business’ end-users (each, a “Patient”).

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the premises and the mutual covenants herein contained, the parties hereto agree as follows:

1. Provider’s Obligations.

a.
Provider shall make available licensed clinicians who are trained and qualified to provide professional services pursuant to this Agreement (each, a “Clinician”), including but not limited to providing remote healthcare services and prescription management services. Such Clinicians will evaluate Patients and determine whether treatment via telemedicine is appropriate based on the Patient’s medical circumstances, applicable law and medical standards, or whether the Patient should be referred to in-person care.
b.
For the duration of this Agreement, Provider shall ensure that a Clinician is available to promptly review and appropriately respond to each telemedicine visit assigned to Provider.
c.
Provider shall ensure that each Clinician is duly licensed in the state(s) in which such Clinician conducts telemedicine visits.
d.
All decisions regarding clinical care shall be based solely upon the professional medical judgment of Provider and its Clinicians and shall be made in the best interests of the Patients. Provider shall, at all times during the Term, have ultimate authority, control, and direction over all clinical care of the Parties’ mutual Patients. Provider shall be solely responsible for all medical, professional, and ethical matters concerning its Clinicians. Provider professional responsibilities include, without limitation, the right and obligation to determine what documentation is necessary for a Clinician to make a decision about the treatment of Patients. Provider and its Clinicians shall not contact or solicit Patients other than for purposes of (i) providing professional services pursuant to the terms of this Agreement or (ii) as otherwise required by law. Provider shall maintain Patients’ medical records in accordance with applicable federal and state laws pertaining to data privacy and security.
e.
Provider and its Clinicians shall comply with and stay up to date on: (i) applicable ethical standards established by the medical and healthcare professions; (ii) applicable clinical standards and protocols established by the medical and healthcare professions; and (iii) applicable healthcare laws and regulations.
f.
Insurance. Provider represents and warrants that it, and each Clinician, has obtained and will maintain throughout the Term professional malpractice insurance coverage from a commercially reasonable, U.S.-based carrier in a minimum amount of $1,000,000 per occurrence and $3,000,000 in the aggregate.

2. Business’ Obligations.

a.
Business will obtain from Patients all legally required (as advised by Provider) informed consents for treatment and consents related to health data collection on behalf of Provider and transmit such consents securely to Provider.
b.
Business will enable Patients to connect with Provider and its assigned Clinician(s), collect relevant information and data from Patients, collect orders, and facilitate order or prescription fulfillment. It shall be Business’ obligation to collect payments from patients and to pay Provider for its services. Business shall also maintain the Website and conduct all branding and marketing efforts.
c.
Business shall comply with all applicable state and Federal laws and regulations regarding the collection, use and disclosure of a Website user’s personal information, including laws and regulations regarding the privacy and security of medical information, where applicable.
d.
Business is not a licensed medical provider. Accordingly, Business will act solely as a navigator for the provision of healthcare services. Website users seeking healthcare services through the Website will be directed to Provider, and Provider or its agents will establish a direct provider/patient relationship with the Website users who become Patients based their eligibility determined solely by the Provider based on its professional judgment. Business will not, and cannot, establish a provider/patient relationship with Patients, and it will not participate in any healthcare decisions regarding the Patients.

3. Confidentiality and HIPAA.

a.
Definition. In connection with this Agreement, each Party (a “Disclosing Party”) may disclose confidential or proprietary information to the other Party (a “Receiving Party”). Subject to the exceptions listed below, “Confidential Information” means information disclosed by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party under this Agreement and during its term that is actually treated by the Disclosing Party as confidential and either: (i) is clearly marked or otherwise clearly designated as confidential or proprietary; (ii) is described in this Section 3(a); or (iii) should be reasonably understood by the Receiving Party to be the confidential or proprietary information of the Disclosing Party.
b.
Exceptions. Confidential Information shall not include any information that: (i) is or becomes publicly available through no wrongful act of the Receiving Party; (ii) the Receiving Party can demonstrate was in the Receiving Party’s possession free of any obligation of confidentiality at the time of the Disclosing Party’s communication of it to the Receiving Party; (iii) becomes known to the Receiving Party directly or indirectly from a source other than one having an obligation of confidentiality; or (iv) is developed by the Receiving Party completely independent from the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party. Notwithstanding any provision in this Section to the contrary, in the event that Receiving Party becomes obligated by mandatory applicable law, regulatory rule or judicial or administrative order to disclose Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, or any portion thereof, Receiving Party shall notify Disclosing Party, so that the Disclosing Party may seek an appropriate protective order or other, similar remedy or relief with respect to resisting or narrowing the scope of such disclosure. In the absence of such a protective order or other remedy or relief, Receiving Party may disclose such Confidential Information, provided that Receiving Party furnishes only such portion of the Confidential Information as it is legally required to disclose.
c.
Obligations of Confidentiality. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of three (3) years after its expiration or termination, each Party shall keep the Confidential Information of the other Party in strict confidence and neither Party may use or otherwise disclose the other Party’s Confidential Information to any third Party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party. Each Party may use and disclose the Confidential Information of the other Party to its employees or subcontractors to the limited extent necessary to permit such Party to perform its obligations under the Agreement, provided that each third party to whom Receiving Party discloses such Confidential Information is bound by obligations of confidentiality with respect to such Confidential Information that are consistent with this Section. Upon the termination of this Agreement, each Party shall return to the other Party or destroy all Confidential Information of such other Party in its possession, custody, or control.
d.
HIPAA. The Parties agree to comply with all applicable federal and state laws and regulations relating to the maintenance, uses and disclosures of protected health information, including without limitation the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as codified at 42 USC § 1320d through d-8 (“HIPAA”). The Parties agree that Provider is a Covered Entity and Business is a Business Associate under HIPAA, and each Party shall comply with its respective obligations under the Business Associate Agreement attached hereto as Exhibit A and incorporated herein (the “BAA”).
e.
Equitable Relief. Both Parties agree that any breach of the confidentiality obligations under this Section may result in irreparable damage for which there is no adequate remedy at law. Therefore, it is agreed that the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, without the necessity of posting a bond or other undertaking, including permanent injunctive relief enjoining such breach, by a court of competent jurisdiction, in addition to whatever remedies it may have at law.

5. Fees.

a.
Visit Fee. Provider shall be paid a visit fee for each patient encounter conducted by Provider (a “Visit Fee”). The amount of the Visit Fee for each of type (if variable) of encounter shall be determined during Parties integration onboarding. All such Visit Fees are exclusive of any taxes, VAT, sales, use, or excise taxes imposed on the sale or use of the Provider’s services, all of which shall be borne exclusively by Business.
b.
Fee Collection and Monthly Report. Utilizing Bask’s software, the Provider will collect fees from Patients and provide to Business a monthly report of total visits by type. Within 30 days of such report, Business will remit payment to Provider via the Bask platform.
c.
Third-Party Terms. The Parties acknowledge and agree that as users and customers of Bask’s software services they are each subject to terms and conditions, policies and other any applicable agreements with Bask that may dictate certain terms of the Parties’ integration, including protocols related to invoicing, payouts, payment processing and payment methods. The Parties further acknowledge and agree that, via Bask or otherwise, the Parties may access and use the services of additional third-parties and, therefore, may be subject to the specific terms and conditions of such third-parties. It is each Party’s responsibility to understand and comply with any such additional terms and conditions, as may be applicable.

6. Term and Termination.

a.
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for one (1) year (the “Initial Term”). Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year terms unless either Party provides notice of non-renewal at least 30 days prior to the termination of the then current Term (each, a “Renewal Term”). The Initial Term and all Renewal Terms are, collectively, the “Term”.
b.
Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience with sixty (60) days’ notice for any or no reason.
c.
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately if the other Party fails to perform or materially observe any of its obligations hereunder or otherwise breaches any representation or warranty hereunder.
d.
Termination of this Agreement by either Party shall not limit or affect any rights and remedies of law or equity or otherwise a Party may have which accrued from events occurring prior to such termination date.

Business Associate Agreement

In accordance with the regulations set forth in 45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164 issued pursuant to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (“HIPAA”) and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (“HITECH”) Act set forth in 42 U.S.C. § 17921 et seq., Provider (as identified in the Bask Health platform provider profile) (“Covered Entity”) and Business (as identified in the Bask Health platform business profile) (“Business Associate”) hereby enter into this Business Associate Agreement (this “BAA”) as of the date on which Business completes click-through acceptance of the Provider Group Services Agreement via the Bask Health platform (the “Effective Date”). Covered Entity and Business Associate are sometimes hereinafter referred to individually as a “Party” and collectively as the “Parties.”

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a “Covered Entity,” as defined in HIPAA;

WHEREAS, HIPAA requires covered entities to protect the privacy of “Protected Health Information” (as defined below) by entering into agreements with persons and entities providing services for covered entities that involve the use or disclosure of protected health information;

WHEREAS, Business Associate is directly subject to HITECH and certain HIPAA provisions;

WHEREAS, Business Associate has been engaged by Covered Entity to provide certain software tools and services that involve the use or disclosure of Protected Health Information (the “Services”) on behalf of Covered Entity;

WHEREAS, in the event that Business Associate is asked to provide the Services on behalf of Covered Entity, Business Associate shall serve as a “Business Associate,” as defined in HIPAA;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to conduct their business relationship in a manner consistent with the requirements of HIPAA and HITECH.

NOW THEREFORE, in exchange for good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree to incorporate the forgoing recitals as rewritten herein and further agree as follows:

1. Definitions.

a.
Generally. The following terms used in this BAA shall have the same meaning as those terms in the HIPAA Rules (as defined below): Breach, Data Aggregation, Designated Record Set, Disclosure, Health Care Operations, Individual, Minimum Necessary, Notice of Privacy Practices, Protected Health Information, Required By Law, Secretary, Security Incident, Subcontractor, Unsecured Protected Health Information, Use, and Workforce.
b.
Business Associate. “Business Associate” shall generally have the same meaning as the term “business associate” at 45 C.F.R. §160.103.
c.
Covered Entity. “Covered Entity” shall generally have the same meaning as the term “covered entity” at 45 C.F.R. §160.103.
d.
HIPAA Rules. “HIPAA Rules” shall mean the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 C.F.R. Part 160 and Part 164.
e.
Protected Health Information. “Protected Health Information” or “PHI” shall generally have the same meaning as the term “protected health information” at 45 C.F.R. §160.103, but in reference to this BAA shall be limited to PHI of the Covered Entity, including PHI created by Business Associate on behalf of the Covered Entity.
f.
Subcontractor. “Subcontractor” shall generally have the same meaning as the term “subcontractor” at 45 C.F.R. §160.103.

2. Obligations of Business Associate.

a.
Use or Disclosure of Information.
i.
Business Associate shall not Use or Disclose Protected Health Information other than as required to perform the Services or as Required By Law. Moreover, Business Associate shall at all times comply with the provisions of the HIPAA Rules applicable to Business Associate.
ii.
Business Associate agrees to limit Uses and Disclosures and requests for Protected Health Information to the Minimum Necessary.
iii.
Business Associate shall not Use or Disclose Protected Health Information in a manner that would violate Subpart E of 45 C.F.R. Part 164 if done by Covered Entity except for the specific Uses and Disclosures set forth in subparagraphs (iv), (v), and (vi) below.
iv.
Business Associate may Use Protected Health Information for the proper management and administration of the Business Associate or to carry out the Business Associate’s legal responsibilities.
v.
Business Associate may Disclose Protected Health Information for the proper management and administration of Business Associate or to carry out the Business Associate’s legal responsibilities, provided the Disclosures are Required by Law, or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the person to whom the information is Disclosed that the information shall remain confidential and Used or further Disclosed only as Required by Law or for the purposes for which it was Disclosed to the person, and the person notifies Business Associate of any instances of which it is aware in which the confidentiality of the information has been Breached.
vi.
Business Associate may provide Data Aggregation services relating to the Health Care Operations of the Covered Entity.
b.
Safeguards. Business Associate shall use appropriate administrative, physical and technical safeguards, and comply with Subpart C of 45 C.F.R. Part 164 with respect to electronic Protected Health Information, to prevent Use or Disclosure of Protected Health Information other than as provided for by this BAA, including, without limitation, appropriate training and discipline of Business Associate’s Workforce and restrictions on access to Protected Health Information.
c.
Mitigation. Business Associate shall immediately mitigate any harmful effect resulting from Use or Disclosure of Protected Health Information by Business Associate, or its Subcontractors or agents, in violation of the requirements of this BAA.
d.
Reporting Breaches. Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity upon Business Associate’s discovery of a Breach within ten (10) days of Business Associate’s discovery of such Breach. Such notice shall include the identification of each individual whose Unsecured Protected Health Information has been or is reasonably believed by Business Associate to have been, accessed, acquired, or disclosed during such Breach. Business Associate shall reasonably cooperate with and assist Covered Entity in making notification to third parties as required by the HIPAA Rules in the event of a Breach due solely to Business Associate.
e.
Reporting Noncompliance. Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any:
i.
Use or Disclosure of Protected Health Information not expressly provided for by this BAA within ten (10) days of Business Associate’s discovery of such Use or Disclosure; and
ii.
Any Security Incident of which Business Associate becomes aware in the following manner: (a) any actual, successful Security Incident shall be reported to Covered Entity in writing, after reasonable investigation by Business Associate; and (b) any attempted, unsuccessful Security Incident, of which Business Associate becomes aware, shall be reported to Entity in writing, on a reasonable basis.
f.
Subcontractors and Agents. In accordance with 45 C.F.R. §164.502(e)(1)(ii) and 164.308(b)(2), Business Associate shall ensure that any Subcontractors or agents that create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information agree in writing to the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements that apply to the Business Associate.
g.
Access, Accounting, Amendments, Compliance With Investigations, and Subpoenas. Business Associate shall comply with the requirements in the BAA text provided above regarding access, disclosures accounting, amendment support, regulatory investigations, and legal process.

3. Obligations of Covered Entity.

Covered Entity shall provide required notices, restrictions, and permissible request handling in accordance with HIPAA and this BAA.

4. Termination.

This BAA terminates in accordance with the Services agreement and HIPAA obligations for return or destruction of PHI and survival provisions.

5. Miscellaneous.
